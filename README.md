## My Approach

1. **Start with the real problem.** What people describe is rarely what's actually going on. I dig deeper through interviews, observation, and behavioral data.

2. **Understand the mechanics.** What biases are at play? What's creating friction? What would make this easier? This is where behavioral science frameworks come in.

3. **Design something practical.** Every recommendation needs to be implementable. Ideas that stay on slides don't help anyone.

4. **Test before committing.** A/B tests, pilots, small experiments. It's better to learn something doesn't work with 50 users than 50,000.

5. **Measure what matters.** Behavioral outcomes, not just satisfaction scores. Did people actually change what they do?

## Why Behavioral Science?

I've always been more interested in understanding why things work than just knowing that they work. Behavioral science gave me a framework for that curiosity.

Most research tells you what happened. Behavioral science helps you understand why, and more importantly, what to do about it. It's the difference between knowing that users aren't adopting a feature and understanding that they associate its visual complexity with unreliability.

I think there's a real gap between academic behavioral science and practical application in business. A lot of companies know they should "use behavioral insights" but don't really know how. I want to be the person who bridges that gap, turning research into recommendations that actually get implemented and measured.
